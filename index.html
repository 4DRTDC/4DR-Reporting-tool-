Methodology & data notes
  • Daily means yesterday — the most recent fully complete day. WTD and MTD run from the start of the week/month through yesterday, so all three numbers are always "as of end of yesterday" and today's partial data never sneaks in.
  • Revenue / Orders / AOV come from your Shopify "Total sales over time" export. Orders placed under an excluded email address (identified from your Orders export) are subtracted from the Orders count — revenue is unaffected since Shopify's own totals already reflect what was actually charged.
  • Pounds is computed by matching each line item in your Orders export to its weight in the Products catalog (grams ÷ 453.592). Orders under an excluded email, and cancelled orders, are excluded entirely.
  • New / Repeat Customers are real counts, built by tracking each customer's email across every Orders export you've uploaded and flagging their earliest known order as "New." Orders under an excluded email don't count toward either bucket. Accuracy improves as more days accumulate — a customer with history before you started using this dashboard may show as "New" the first time they're seen here.
  • Customer Acquisition Cost = Meta ad spend for the period ÷ New Customers for the same period.
  • Top Performing Ad = highest number of Results (conversions) in the period, from your Meta Ads-by-day export. Ties break toward lower spend. If no ad converted in the period, ranking falls back to highest CTR (Link clicks ÷ Impressions), then highest Impressions, then lowest spend — so a $0 conversion count doesn't default to whichever ad happened to spend the least.
  • Top Selling Product = highest units sold in the period, from your Orders export line items (grouped by product/variant name). Orders under an excluded email, cancelled orders, and "4DR GRD 15" are excluded from ranking.
  • Orders placed under hannah.tredway@fat-earth.com or tcawdrey@fourdiamondranch.com are excluded from every metric on this dashboard.
  • Your Orders export is a rolling window (not full history), so pounds/customer data is stored locally in this browser and merged in day over day, deduped by order number. Use Backup data periodically as a safety net — it saves everything to a file you can restore from.
  • A small ✦ next to a number means the date range extends earlier than your accumulated order history — that figure may be incomplete for the earliest days in range.
Runs entirely in this browser. Nothing is uploaded anywhere — data lives in this browser's local storage until you clear it or use Reset.
